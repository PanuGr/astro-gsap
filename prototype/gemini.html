Kirikla Monastery

The only Orthodox men's monastery in Estonia

A living place of prayer, work, and monastic community — rooted in the tradition of Mount Athos.

Support the Monastery Visit the Monastery

Founded in 2025 under Bishop Damaskinos of Haapsalu, a member of the Xenophontos Monastery brotherhood on Mount Athos, Kirikla Monastery is home to a small brotherhood dedicated to prayer and service. Visitors, pilgrims, and friends are always welcome.

Our History

For centuries, Petseri Monastery stood as Estonia's Orthodox spiritual center, a pillar of faith that ended after independence when the region remained on the Russian side of the border.

In 2024, Hieromonk Damaskinos was elected Bishop of Haapsalu. With his consecration, he brought the deep, unbroken spiritual traditions of Mount Athos to the Estonian faithful.

The vision of a new monastic home materialized in 2026 when entrepreneur Urmas Sõõrumaa gifted property in Kirikla, Harju County, located just 40km from Tallinn. During the initial phases, the brotherhood resides temporarily at the nearby Üiso Farm.

The spiritual foundation of the brotherhood took root in 2025 when the first monk, Father Paisios, was tonsured. He was ordained as a deacon in 2026, a year that also saw a second brother join the community as a novice.

We pray with fervent hope for the continued growth of this young brotherhood.

Life at the Monastery

The monastic day is strictly and reverently divided into prayer, work, and rest.

Prayer

Both communal and private, beginning in the early morning hours to offer the first fruits of the day to God.

Work

Labor sustains the monastery. The monks engage in cleaning, kitchen duties, gardening, icon painting, and construction.

Rest

Rest is deliberately scarce. The monks rise before dawn, ensuring the spiritual vigil of the community remains unbroken.

We live a coenobitic communal life holding no private property. The brotherhood operates as a family, bound tightly by mutual love, obedience, and an ethos deeply rooted in the traditions of Mount Athos.

Visiting

Monasteries are essential places of spiritual retreat. We warmly welcome you to see the chapel, walk the grounds, and find rest for your soul. As we currently have no regular visiting hours, please ensure visits are made by prior arrangement.
